A brief overview of a pairs trade follows. Pairs arbitrage is a mean reversion strategy trading two or more securities: * with co-integrated prices, and * that have temporarily diverged. The strategy involves buying one security whilst simultaneously shorting the other security when the price differential is outside a given measure. On price convergence both positions are closed at profit. Of course, the prices may never re-converge, for example if there is change in the securities' fundamentals or the macroeconomic environment.

Pairs trading opportunities are relatively rare, hence the development of this tool to enable daily observation of potential trends.
